1627

February 19: Shivaji Maharaj was born at Shivneri Fort, Maharashtra, to Shahaji Bhosale and Jijabai.

1636

Shivaji began his formal military training under his father, Shahaji, who served the Bijapur Sultanate.

1645

Capture of Torna Fort: At the age of 16, Shivaji took control of Torna Fort, marking the beginning of his military career.

Occupation of Kondana Fort: Shivaji strategically captured Kondana Fort (later renamed Sinhagad).

1646

Shivaji captured several forts in the Junnar region, including Chakan and Sinhagad.

1647

He consolidated his control over the Pune region and started building an independent kingdom.

1648

Defense of Purandar Fort: Shivaji successfully defended Purandar Fort against a Bijapuri invasion led by Fateh Khan.

1656

Conquest of Javali: Shivaji launched a campaign against the Mores of Javali, gaining control of the strategic fort and surrounding region.

1657

Capture of Rajgad: Shivaji conquered the hill fort of Rajgad, making it his capital and a key stronghold.

1659

Battle of Pratapgarh: Shivaji defeated Afzal Khan, a prominent general of the Bijapur Sultanate, using strategic and psychological warfare.

Capture of Panhala Fort: Shivaji seized the fort of Panhala from Bijapur, further consolidating his power.

1660

Battle of Pavan Khind: Shivaji's forces, led by Baji Prabhu Deshpande, made a heroic last stand against the Bijapuri forces, allowing Shivaji to escape to Vishalgad.

1663

Battle with Shaista Khan: Shaista Khan, a Mughal general, was sent by Aurangzeb to capture Shivaji. Shaista Khan occupied Pune and made Lal Mahal his headquarters. In a daring night raid in 1663, Shivaji and his men infiltrated Lal Mahal, attacked Shaista Khan, and wounded him. Shaista Khan lost three fingers but managed to escape, which was a significant blow to the Mughal pride.

1664

Raid on Surat: Shivaji conducted a daring raid on Surat, a major Mughal trading port, and looted its wealth to fund his military campaigns.

1665

Treaty of Purandar: Following his arrest at Agra and subsequent escape, Shivaji was forced to sign the Treaty of Purandar with the Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb, ceding 23 forts.

1666

Escape from Agra and return to Raigadh.

1667-1670

Consolidation of Power: Shivaji continued to strengthen his empire by reorganizing the administration and military, promoting trade, and building a strong navy to protect the Konkan coast.

1667-1670

Consolidation of Power: Shivaji continued to strengthen his empire by reorganizing the administration and military, promoting trade, and building a strong navy to protect the Konkan coast.

1674

Coronation: Shivaji Maharaj was crowned Chhatrapati on June 6 at Raigad Fort, formally establishing the Maratha Empire. The coronation ceremony was a grand affair, signifying his sovereignty and the recognition of his kingdom.

1675

Campaign in the South: Shivaji launched a southern campaign, successfully capturing key forts and territories in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka, including Tanjore and Vellore.

1677

Shivaji entered into an alliance with Golconda Sultanate to counter the Mughal and Bijapur forces. He also secured the coastal regions of Andhra Pradesh.

1678

Naval Strengthening: Shivaji focused on strengthening the Maratha navy to protect against European colonial powers and improve maritime trade.

1679

Battle of Sangamner Fought between the Mughal Empire and Maratha Empire in 1679. This was the last battle in which the Shivaji Maharaj fought.

1680

April 3: Shivaji Maharaj passed away at Raigad Fort. His death marked the end of an era, but his legacy continued through his successors and the resilient Maratha Empire.
